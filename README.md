# Global Food Security
### Systemic Analysis to Address Hunger

---
#### Objective: Raise awareness of the global food crisis, identify its systemic causes, and propose collaborative solutions to alleviate the crisis

---

<div align="center">
  <img src="images/global_food_insecurity_map.png" alt="Global Food Insecurity Map">
  <br><sub><a href="https://www.fsinplatform.org/sites/default/files/resources/files/GRFC2023-compressed.pdf">The Global Report on Food Crises 2023</a></sub>
</div>

Africa is the most affected region, with 282 million people experiencing hunger, particularly in East Africa, where conditions are deteriorating. The risk of famine in the region is comparable to or even worse than the Ethiopian famine of the 1980s, with 7.2 million people at risk of starvation and another 26.5 million facing acute food insecurity.

<div align="center">
  <img src="https://github.com/patmejia/global_food_analysis/assets/92187562/9f988660-3127-4a72-bf0a-23a28b030c1f" alt="agriculture-food-security-east-africa discoveries">
  <br><sub>Aug 2020 - Dec 2022. Resource: <a href="https://github.com/NASA-IMPACT/veda-config">NASA-IMPACT VEDA Dashboard</a> | <a href="https://www.earthdata.nasa.gov/dashboard/stories/agriculture-food-security-east-africa">Concern About Food Security in Eastern Africa - OCT 19, 2022</a></sub>
</div>

The global hunger and malnutrition crisis has escalated in 2023, affecting over 811 million people with acute food insecurity, more than double the number from 2019.

<div align="center">
  <img src="images/global_number_food_insecurity.png" alt="Alt text">
  <br><sub>World Bank | <a href="https://thedocs.worldbank.org/en/doc/40ebbf38f5a6b68bfc11e5273e1405d4-0090012022/related/Food-Security-Update-XC-July-27-2023.pdf">Food Security Update</a></sub>
</div>

This surge is driven by a phenomenon called ‚Äúseismic hunger‚Äù, which refers to a sudden and large-scale increase in hunger and food insecurity due to a combination of factors:

<div align="center">
  <img src="images/primary_driver_of_food_insecurity.png" alt="Drivers of Food Insecurity">
  <br><sub>Food Insecurity Drivers. Resource: <a href="https://www.fsinplatform.org/sites/default/files/resources/files/GRFC2023-compressed.pdf">The Global Report on Food Crises 2023</a></sub>
</div>

*1.*  **Conflict and Economic Shocks**: Ukraine conflict, which disrupted food production and trade in Eastern Europe and Central Asia

<div align="center">
  <img src="images/largest_currency_depreciation.png" alt="Currency Depreciation and Food Insecurity">
  <br><sub>Currencies Depreciation. Resource: <a href="https://www.fsinplatform.org/sites/default/files/resources/files/GRFC2023-compressed.pdf">The Global Report on Food Crises 2023</a></sub>
</div>

*2.* **Climate Change**: Repeated failing rains in East Africa, which left over 7 million people at risk of starvation.

<div align="center">
  <img src="images/cause_worst_drought_Ethhiopia.png" alt="Alt text">
</div>

*3.* **Soaring Food and Fuel Prices**: Global increases affecting availability and affordability of food, especially for low-income households.

<div align="center">
  <img src="images/food_and_energy_prices.png" alt="Alt text">
</div>

*4.* **COVID-19 Pandemic**: Lingering effects on global food supply chains.

<div align="center">
  <img src="images/trading_2023_global_updated.png" alt="Alt text">
  <br><sub>International Monetary. The International Monetary Fund (IMF), Haver Analytics, and Trading Economics</sub>
</div>

The 2023 global food crisis is not merely a set of statistics; it represents a human tragedy on a massive scale. Understanding the complexity of ‚Äúseismic hunger‚Äù is crucial in formulating strategies to combat it. The response must be immediate, robust, and well-coordinated, reflecting the multifaceted nature of the crisis itself.
